How To Use The Backtest System

Step-by-step guide to validating and improving your market analyzer

1 Run a Replay Backtest

  • Go to your Replay Backtest page in the terminal app
  • Select a time range - pick periods with varied market conditions (up, down, sideways)
  • Choose step size: 1h for macro analysis, 30m for scalping
  • Set horizon: MICRO = 2-8 hours, MACRO = 1-5 days
  • Click "Run Backtest" and wait for completion

üí° Tip: Start with at least 7 days of data to get meaningful patterns. Aim for 200+ snapshots.

2 Label the Outcomes

  • After replay completes, click "Label Outcomes"
  • This looks at what actually happened after each prediction
  • Labels: CONTINUATION (bias was right), REVERSAL (bias was wrong), NOISE (no significant move)

3 Analyze Your Results

  • Check Overall Accuracy: % of CONTINUATION / (CONTINUATION + REVERSAL)
  • Check Directional Accuracy: LONG accuracy vs SHORT accuracy
  • Check Confidence Calibration: Does confidence=8 beat confidence=5?
  • Check Regime Performance: Which regimes are failing?

4 Identify the Dominant Failure

  • Look at your error bucketing - which category has most failures?
  • Pull 5-10 specific examples from that category
  • Ask: What conditions led to this error?
  • Propose a single fix (don't fix multiple things!)

üí° Key Principle: Fix ONE thing at a time. If you change multiple things, you won't know what worked.

5 Make the Change & Re-Test

  • Document baseline: Save current metrics before changing anything
  • Make ONE change: Adjust threshold, weight, or logic
  • Run unit tests: Make sure nothing broke
  • Re-run backtest: Same parameters as before
  • Compare: Did metrics improve? Did anything regress?
